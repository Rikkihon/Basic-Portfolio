    About Me

    In 2014, I began my new career in Tech Support, and then expanded into coding over the successive years. I've taken the U of Minnesota Coding Bootcamp Program and completed classes in Java and Python and discrete math at Metro State University.
    In Java, we covered polymorphism and encapsulation and I still dream of "public static main void."
    I speak a little bit of Japanese; I can read Hiragana and Katakaa, but I know a bit more of Spanish than Japanese. I'm a foster parent and I love downhill skiing on Sunday night at Buckhill for their $10 Sunday Special. Once upon my past, I used to do glamorous things like "Go to Spin Class", "Read actual books", and that thing they call "naps".
    My new hobbies include managing Type 1 diabetes in a toddler and navigating the meetings foster parents must attend for the county.
